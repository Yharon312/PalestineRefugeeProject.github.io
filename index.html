Palestinian Refugees

An informational overview

About This Website

This website currently provides an overview of Palestinian refugees, their history, current status, and living conditions. our goal is to raise awareness and provide accurate information about this important topic. This is a prototype made by Troy Coulter, Sid Belt Gadgil, and Kristthor Gudnason for our world literature class on the subject of the book Kite Runner at Innovation Academy.

Background

Palestinian refugees are one of the world's longest-standing refugee populations. Their displacement began in 1948 during the events known as the Nakba ("catastrophe"), when approximately 700,000 Palestinians were displaced from their homes. Subsequent conflicts produced further waves of displacement.

Key Statistics

  • About 5.9 million registered refugees under UNRWA (as of 2023)
  • 58 official refugee camps across the region
  • Many refugees are now third or fourth generation

Living Conditions

Many refugees live in overcrowded camps with limited access to clean water, sanitation, and healthcare. UNRWA (the UN Relief and Works Agency) has provided education, health, and relief services since 1950.
